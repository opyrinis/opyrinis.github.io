Project Summary

This project is a 2D Grid Based Fluid Dynamics Simulation. Features include a pressure map, velocity map, and interactions through adding velocity and colors. Some optional tasks will be to implement a viscosity slider, a density map, and simulate gravity.


Problem Description

Fluids are all around us, as the water we drink and as the oil in our cars. People have long studied the dynamics of fluids around us, and one way to study them is through visualization. For this project, we have decided to create a 2D fluid simulation. Simulations, or visualizations in general, allow people to understand the object being visualized better while being interesting and aesthetically pleasing.

In this project, we want to simulate the motion of 2D fluid over time, with changing velocity and pressure. We plan to illustrate changes in velocity, pressure, and color, then add some more features as time permits. The challenges in this project will be how to approximate solutions to differential equations in discrete time and visualize them in real time.


Goals and Deliverables (What we Plan to Deliver)

We plan to have an application that simulates real time Fluid Dynamics in 2D. As a baseline, we plan to have a UI that can toggle between a pressure and velocity map of a simulated fluid. The fluid will have some initial velocity for every pixel that the user can set. We also want the user to be able to drop multiple colors of dye on a pixel or group of pixels into the simulation to watch them dissipate as they are affected by the velocities. We plan to check the quality of our simulation visually through the maps, as well as making sure it is updating at 30 fps (real time).

To implement the fluid dynamics simulation, we will be utilizing a 2D velocity field grid which, for each pixel, the velocity vector of the fluid will be updated for each predetermined, small time step. In order to calculate the new velocity vectors for each pixel in the field map, we will be employing the Helmholtz-Hodge Decomposition of the Navier-Stokes equations which take into consideration fluid properties such as: the pressure map from the previous time step, the velocity map from the previous time step, and the viscosity of the fluid.

Some question we plan to answer in the analysis are:

  • How do initial conditions affect how the simulation evolves with time?
  • How does pressure affect the movement of the fluids?
  • How does pressure affect velocity, and how does velocity affect pressure?
  • How do the different colors mix together with different initial conditions?
  • How does varying the properties of the fluids (viscosity, density, etc) affect the simulation?


Goals and Deliverables (What we Hope to Deliver)

If we are able to complete the baseline tasks ahead of time, we plan to add other parameters to the simulation, like gravity, viscosity, and compressibility. If we are feeling the challenge, we can also add a temperature map to show how viscosity and gravity would affect temperature.

Other stretch goals:

  • Allow the browser window to be moved, such that the movement will affect the fluids inside.
  • Add compressible fluids and show a density and temperature map of the simulated fluids.
  • Simulate multiple fluids simultaneously and observe their interactions with one another.
